__William (Bill) D. Phillips__ is a quantum physicist at the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), as well as a Distinguished University Professor and College Park Professor of Physics at the University of Maryland. He shared the 1997 Nobel Prize in physics for cooling and trapping atoms using lasers. Bill also is a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, the American Association for the Advancement of Science, and the National Academy of Sciences. He has engaged in diversity and public-outreach efforts for many years, earning the NIST Equal Opportunity/Diversity Award in 2011. Bill presents public lectures across the globe, including at minority-serving institutions and elementary schools. Closer to home, he mentors undergraduates at Morgan State University, encouraging physics majors to stay the course by sharing excitement about science.
